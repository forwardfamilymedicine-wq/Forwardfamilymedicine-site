## Quick Answer
Concierge and Direct Primary Care practices are growing along the Main Line and Philadelphia suburbs, offering busy professionals and families an alternative to rushed, hard-to-access conventional primary care. Forward Family Medicine in Wayne, PA brings this model to the heart of the Main Line.

## Explanation

The Philadelphia Main Line — the string of communities running northwest from the city along the old Pennsylvania Railroad corridor through Bryn Mawr, Wayne, Berwyn, Paoli, and beyond — has always been home to people with high expectations. High-achieving professionals, dual-income families, executives managing demanding schedules, and retirees who've spent decades building something worth protecting. These are people who are accustomed to finding the best option available and who understand the value of investing in things that matter.

And yet, for all of that, the primary care experience for most Main Line residents is indistinguishable from the primary care experience anywhere else in the country: a three-week wait for a routine appointment, a 40-minute sit in the waiting room with a magazine from 2023, and then 12 minutes with a physician who is already typing their next note before you finish your sentence.

It doesn't have to be that way. And increasingly, Main Line patients are discovering that it isn't.

**The Concierge Model Arrives in the Suburbs**

Concierge medicine and Direct Primary Care have expanded well beyond their early reputation as urban, elite-only offerings. Today, DPC practices in particular — which offer the concierge experience at a flat monthly fee without insurance billing — are operating throughout the Philadelphia suburbs. They're serving professionals, families, retirees, self-employed individuals, and anyone else who has decided that their primary care relationship is worth investing in.

The Main Line is a natural fit for this model. The demographics here — high rates of employer-sponsored insurance alongside significant self-employment and small business ownership, a culture of valuing expertise and access, and a geography where people are accustomed to driving five or ten minutes to get exactly what they're looking for — align well with what DPC delivers.

**What Main Line Patients Should Look for in a Concierge Practice**

If you're evaluating concierge or DPC practices in the Wayne, Radnor, Villanova, or broader Main Line area, a few criteria matter more than others:

*Panel size.* The most important number in any concierge practice is how many patients the physician currently sees. A practice with 600 patients can offer meaningfully better access than conventional care. But panel size matters along the full spectrum. At Forward Family Medicine, we cap our panel at approximately 250 patients per physician — roughly half the DPC average — because we believe that number is what genuine availability and true continuity actually require. When we say same-day access, we mean it structurally, not aspirationally.

*Physician continuity.* One of the primary values of concierge care is that you have a consistent relationship with one physician — not a rotating cast of providers. Ask how the practice handles physician absence, and make sure you understand who you'll be seeing.

*Clinical depth* — specifically, whether the practice offers integrated Lifestyle Medicine. Forward Family Medicine is the only DPC practice on the Main Line with in-person, board-certified Lifestyle Medicine (Dr. Lieberman, dual-certified in Family Medicine and Lifestyle Medicine). This means nutrition, physical activity, sleep, and stress management are treated as clinical interventions — not handouts — and included in every membership at no additional cost.

*What the fee actually covers.* This varies more than you'd expect. Some DPC practices include labs; others offer wholesale pricing. Some include telehealth; others charge separately. Get a clear picture of the full scope before you compare prices.

*Geographic access and convenience.* For Main Line residents, a practice in Wayne is accessible whether you're coming from Radnor, Berwyn, Malvern, or Villanova. SEPTA's Paoli/Thorndale Regional Rail line runs through Wayne, and the practice is reachable by car from throughout Delaware and Chester counties.

**Why DPC Serves Main Line Professionals Particularly Well**

Many Main Line professionals are self-employed, own small businesses, or work in industries where their health directly affects their productivity. For this population, the economics of DPC paired with a high-deductible health plan often make more sense than a premium PPO, and the access model — same-day appointments, direct physician messaging, telehealth — fits their actual lives better than a practice that requires booking three weeks out.

The Main Line also has a significant population of patients managing chronic conditions alongside busy schedules: hypertension, diabetes, thyroid disorders, cardiac risk factors. DPC's model of unlimited primary care visits for a flat fee is purpose-built for ongoing chronic disease management without the financial friction of copays discouraging necessary follow-up.

## Practical Tips

- When researching concierge or DPC practices on the Main Line, search specifically for physician-owned independent practices — these tend to maintain smaller panels and more genuine physician access than practices owned by hospital systems.
- Ask whether the physician lives or practices locally and has a long-term commitment to the area — continuity of the practice matters as much as continuity of care.
- If you work in Philadelphia and live on the Main Line, consider whether the practice can accommodate telehealth during the workday — a major convenience for city workers.
- For families, look for DPC practices that see patients of all ages — having the whole family with one physician simplifies care coordination considerably.
- SEPTA Regional Rail access to Wayne makes the practice reachable without a car if you need it.
- Ask prospective practices about their approach to care coordination with Philadelphia-area specialists at Penn, Jefferson, Main Line Health, and Lankenau — this network knowledge matters.

**Q: Is there a DPC or concierge practice that serves Wayne, Radnor, Villanova, and surrounding Main Line communities?**

**Q: Do Main Line concierge practices accept insurance at all?**
A: It depends on the model. Traditional concierge practices usually still accept and bill insurance on top of a retainer fee. DPC practices like Forward Family Medicine do not bill insurance for primary care — the monthly membership covers primary care services. Patients still maintain their own insurance for specialist care, hospitalizations, and other services outside the practice's scope.

**Q: Can I join a DPC practice if my employer provides insurance?**
A: Absolutely. DPC membership is compatible with any insurance plan. Many DPC patients have employer-sponsored insurance and use it for specialists, imaging, and hospital care, while their DPC membership covers all primary care needs. Some employers are beginning to offer DPC memberships as a supplemental benefit — worth asking your HR department about.

**Q: How is a Main Line DPC practice different from going to Penn Medicine or Main Line Health for primary care?**
A: Large health system primary care practices operate under the same constraints as any conventional practice — large patient panels, time pressure, insurance billing requirements. DPC is structurally different: smaller panel, no insurance billing overhead, and a model designed around patient access and relationship. The care philosophy is different, not just the price structure.
