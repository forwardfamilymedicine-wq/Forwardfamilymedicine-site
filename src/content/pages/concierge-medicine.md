## What Is Concierge Medicine?

Concierge medicine (also called boutique medicine, retainer medicine, or personalized medicine) is an approach to primary care in which patients pay a membership or retainer fee in exchange for enhanced access and a deeper relationship with their physician.

The core promise of concierge medicine is simple: **a doctor with time for you.**

### Hallmarks of Concierge-Style Care
- Small patient panel — your doctor knows you by name and history
- Same-day or next-day appointment availability
- Direct phone, text, or email access to your physician
- Longer, more thorough appointments (30–60 minutes)
- Proactive, prevention-focused care — not just reactive sick visits
- Coordination with specialists and advocacy on your behalf
- 24/7 or after-hours urgent access to your own physician

Forward Family Medicine offers all of these — built on the Direct Primary Care (DPC) model.

## The Concierge Experience at Forward Family Medicine

### Your Doctor's Personal Attention
Dr. Bigley and Dr. Lieberman maintain a purposefully small panel of members. That means every patient receives the kind of attention that simply isn't possible in a practice seeing 20–30 patients a day.

When you call, you speak directly with your doctor or a team member who knows your name. When you send a message, you receive a thoughtful response — not a portal notification pointing you back to schedule an appointment.

### Same-Day Access — Really
Urgent concern on a Monday morning? We aim for same-day availability for acute issues. Routine visits can typically be scheduled within 24 hours. For many questions, a quick phone call or message means you never need to come in at all.

### Visits Without a Clock
Appointments at Forward Family Medicine are scheduled to fit the conversation — not to hit a billing threshold. A routine medication refill might take 10 minutes. A new diagnosis, a complex chronic condition, or a patient who just needs to talk things through might take an hour. We make room for both.

### A Practice That Actively Lowers Your Costs

Concierge medicine has a reputation for being expensive. We think it should be the opposite. Because we know you well and you can reach us directly, we help you avoid the urgent care visits, redundant tests, and reactive ER trips that quietly inflate the cost of traditional care. We also dispense medications at cost and order labs at wholesale pricing — so when something is needed, it's not a financial event. The goal is for your total annual healthcare spend to go down as an FFM member, not up.

### Proactive Prevention
Concierge-style care means we do not wait for you to get sick to engage. We schedule preventive health reviews, track your labs over time, follow up on recommendations we've made, and reach out when we notice something worth discussing.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Is concierge medicine worth it?**
Most patients who have experienced both traditional and concierge-style care say it is — particularly when the total cost (including copays, deductibles, and the value of time saved) is factored in. Our DPC model keeps the membership fee low enough that the math works for most families.

**Does Forward Family Medicine bill insurance?**
We do not bill insurance for primary care services. Your monthly membership fee covers all included services. You should maintain health insurance for specialist, hospital, and emergency care.

**How small is the patient panel really?**
We keep our panel purposefully limited to ensure every member receives the attention they deserve. Once the panel is full, we maintain a waitlist. We encourage prospective members to [reach out early →](/become-a-patient).

**Can I keep my current insurance and add this membership?**
Yes. Most members combine our DPC membership with their existing insurance (or switch to a high-deductible plan) for specialist and hospital coverage.

**What if I'm used to a large health system and want to keep those relationships?**
We work collaboratively with specialist physicians and large health systems. You do not need to abandon those relationships — you simply add a primary care physician who is far more accessible and engaged.
