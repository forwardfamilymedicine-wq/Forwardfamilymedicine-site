# Become a Patient at Forward Family Medicine

## Hero

**Your first step costs nothing — just a conversation.**

Before you commit to anything, we invite you to a free, no-obligation Meet & Greet with Dr. Bigley and Dr. Lieberman. Ask every question you have, see the practice, and decide — on your timeline, with no pressure — whether Forward Family Medicine is the right fit for you and your family.

## How to Get Started

### Step 1 — Schedule a Free Meet & Greet {#schedule}

A 20-minute conversation — in person or via video — with Dr. Bigley and Dr. Lieberman. This is your chance to:

- Ask anything about the practice, the DPC model, and what membership includes
- Share your health history and current concerns to see if we're a good fit
- Tour the practice if you're coming in person
- Meet the team and get a feel for the culture

**No paperwork. No commitment. Just a conversation.**

### Step 2 — Enroll Online

If you decide to become a member, enrollment is simple and takes about 10 minutes online.

1. Complete the enrollment form (personal information, health history, insurance details for reference)
2. Set up your monthly membership billing (ACH bank transfer or credit card)
3. Sign the membership agreement — a plain-language document explaining exactly what's included

> **Enrollment form link placeholder:** `[LINK: Patient enrollment portal]`

---

### Step 3 — Your First Comprehensive Visit

Once enrolled, we schedule your onboarding visit — a 60-minute appointment where we:

- Review your full health history in depth
- Discuss your health goals and any active concerns
- Order any overdue labs or screenings
- Build your personalized care plan, including lifestyle medicine components
- Set up your direct access to Dr. Bigley and Dr. Lieberman

This is the most thorough first appointment most patients have experienced. Come with questions — we have time for all of them.

---

### Step 4 — You're a Member

From day one of membership, you have direct access to Dr. Bigley and Dr. Lieberman by phone, text, and secure message. Same-day and next-day appointments are available. Your care has begun.

## What to Expect at Your Meet & Greet

The Meet & Greet is informal and unhurried. Dr. Bigley and Dr. Lieberman will ask about your current health situation and what brings you in, and you'll have time to ask about:

- How to transition from your current doctor
- What a typical year of membership looks like
- Whether your specific conditions and concerns fall within our scope
- How labs and medications are priced
- Anything else on your mind

There is no obligation at any point in the process. If you decide it's not the right fit, we'll part warmly and wish you well.

---

## Transitioning from Another Doctor

We make transitions easy:

- We request records from your previous provider on your behalf
- Ongoing prescriptions are reviewed and continued (where appropriate) from your first visit
- Specialist relationships are maintained — we work with your existing specialists, not against them
- We coordinate any outstanding referrals or care gaps from prior to enrollment

Most patients find the transition takes one or two weeks from enrollment to first visit, depending on how quickly records are available.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Is the Meet & Greet really free?**
Yes, completely. There is no fee and no obligation. It is simply a conversation to help you decide if Forward Family Medicine is right for you.

**What if I'm not ready to schedule — can I just get more information?**
Of course. [Browse our FAQ →](/faq) or [call or email us directly](#contact) with any questions.

**How long until I can get my first real appointment after enrolling?**
Most members have their onboarding visit within one to two weeks of enrollment. If you have an urgent concern before your first visit, contact us and we'll see you sooner.

**Can I enroll my whole family at once?**
Yes. Family plans are available and recommended — having one physician who knows every family member's history is one of the great benefits of a DPC practice.

**What if I decide to leave the practice?**
Month-to-month members can cancel with 30 days' written notice. Annual members may be subject to the terms of their agreement. We do not make it difficult to leave — but we do work hard to make sure you never want to.
