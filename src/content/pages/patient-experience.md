# The Patient Experience at Forward Family Medicine

## Hero

**Primary care that feels like it used to — and better than you expected.**

Being a member at Forward Family Medicine is a different kind of healthcare experience. No waiting rooms full of strangers. No hold music. No 8-minute appointments. Just a physician who knows you, is available when you need them, and has the time to actually help.

Here's what membership looks like in real life.

---

## From the First Conversation

### The Free Meet & Greet
Before you become a member, we invite you to a free, no-obligation Meet & Greet with Dr. Bigley and Dr. Lieberman. This is a 20-minute conversation — in the office or by video — where you can:

- Ask anything about the practice and the DPC model
- Share your health history and current concerns
- Understand exactly what membership includes and how it works
- See the practice space and meet the team
- Decide, with no pressure, whether it's the right fit

Most prospective members say the Meet & Greet answered questions they didn't even know they had. Many leave having already decided to join.

## Becoming a Member

Enrollment is straightforward:

1. **Complete your enrollment form online** — takes about 10 minutes
2. **Set up your membership billing** — one flat monthly charge, no hidden fees
3. **Submit your health history** — via our secure patient portal
4. **Request records from your previous provider** — we help facilitate this

Once enrolled, you receive access to Dr. Bigley and Dr. Lieberman's direct contact information. From day one, you can reach out by phone, text, or message.

---

## Your First Comprehensive Visit

New members receive a **60-minute onboarding visit** — the most thorough first appointment most patients have ever had.

We cover:
- Your full personal and family health history
- Current medications and supplements
- Preventive health needs and overdue screenings
- Your goals — not just your diagnoses
- Any active concerns you want to address
- A personalized health plan, including lifestyle medicine components

Patients regularly tell us this was the first time a doctor asked about their sleep, their stress, their diet, and what they actually want from their healthcare.

## Day-to-Day Membership: What Access Really Means

### When Something Comes Up
A sore throat on Tuesday morning. A question about a medication side effect. A child who woke up with an ear infection. A blood pressure reading that looks off.

As a member, you don't call a general office line and wait. You send a message or call Dr. Bigley and Dr. Lieberman directly. You get a response the same day — often within hours. If you need to be seen, you're in by end of day or the next morning.

Compare this to the typical experience: calling a practice, being put on hold, speaking to a nurse who takes a message, waiting for a callback, and then being told the next available appointment is in two weeks.

### When You Come In for a Visit
The waiting room at Forward Family Medicine is quiet. There is no double-booking, no overflowing schedule. When you have an appointment, it starts on time.

Visits are as long as they need to be. A medication refill or a quick concern might take 15 minutes. A new diagnosis or a preventive health review might take 45–60 minutes. There is no pressure to hurry.

### Between Visits
You can reach Dr. Bigley and Dr. Lieberman by text, message, or phone for any clinical question. Lab results are reviewed with you directly — not just released to a portal without explanation. Follow-up is proactive: if a concern needs monitoring, we reach out to you, not the other way around.

## Appointments That Feel Different

In a traditional practice, a physician seeing 25 patients a day is spending an average of 15 minutes per person — and much of that time is documentation, not conversation.

At Forward Family Medicine, a typical appointment looks like this:

**You arrive.** The office is quiet and familiar. The staff know your name.

**The visit starts on time.** Dr. Bigley and Dr. Lieberman has reviewed your chart before you walk in — you don't need to repeat your history from scratch.

**You have time to talk.** All of your questions get answered. If something comes up mid-visit that needs more time, we make room for it.

**You leave with a clear plan.** What we found, what it means, and what comes next — explained in plain language.

**You have a way to follow up.** If you have a question at home, you know how to reach Dr. Bigley and Dr. Lieberman directly.

## For People with Chronic Conditions

If you live with a chronic condition — diabetes, hypertension, thyroid disease, anxiety, or something else requiring ongoing management — the DPC model offers something traditional medicine rarely does: time and continuity.

Your condition is managed with the full context of your life. Medication adjustments happen promptly, not at the next available quarterly slot. When you have a flare or a concern between visits, you reach your own doctor — who already knows your full history.

Many members with chronic conditions report better lab values, fewer complications, and — importantly — feeling genuinely supported in managing their health over time.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Is the experience really that different from a traditional practice?**
Yes — meaningfully so. The most consistent feedback from members is that they had forgotten what it felt like to have a doctor who knows them and has time for them. The DPC model makes this structurally possible in a way that traditional insurance-based medicine cannot.

**What if I need to be seen quickly and my doctor is unavailable?**
Dr. Bigley and Dr. Lieberman builds same-day capacity into the schedule every day. For true emergencies, we direct you to the appropriate care setting. For urgent-but-not-emergency concerns when Dr. Bigley and Dr. Lieberman is unavailable, we have a coverage plan in place and will communicate it to you clearly.

**Can I still see specialists I've been seeing for years?**
Yes. Your DPC membership is for primary care. Your specialist relationships remain completely intact. We coordinate with your specialists and can serve as a communication hub.

**Is the office accessible and easy to get to?**
Yes. We're located in [address placeholder], with convenient parking. [See directions →](/become-a-patient#location)

**How do I communicate between visits — what platform do you use?**
We use a HIPAA-compliant messaging platform for secure communication. Details are provided at enrollment. In practice, most members use a combination of text, direct call, and portal message — whatever works best for them.
