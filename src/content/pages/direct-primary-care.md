# What Is Direct Primary Care?

**Direct Primary Care is primary care freed from insurance billing — and built around the patient. At Forward Family Medicine, DPC isn't a budget alternative to concierge medicine. It's the financial structure that makes concierge-quality care available to everyone.**

In a Direct Primary Care (DPC) practice, patients pay a flat monthly membership fee directly to their physician. In return, they receive unlimited primary care services, direct access to their doctor, and longer, unhurried visits. No copays. No billing codes. No insurance middleman for everyday care.

---

## The Short Answer

**Direct Primary Care (DPC)** is a healthcare model in which:
1. Patients pay a predictable monthly membership fee directly to their physician ($200/month for individuals, less per person for families — reflecting a smaller panel and deeper level of care)
2. The practice does **not** bill insurance for primary care services
3. Patients receive **unlimited visits**, direct doctor access, and often **wholesale labs and medications**
4. The physician maintains a **smaller patient panel** — at FFM, capped at 250 patients per physician, versus 2,000+ in traditional practices

The result: your doctor has time for you.

---

## Why Does DPC Exist?

Traditional insurance-based primary care creates a structural problem: physicians are paid per visit, and payers (insurance companies) reimburse more for procedures than for thinking, listening, and preventing disease. To cover overhead, a primary care physician in a traditional practice needs to see 20–30 patients per day.

In that environment, appointments get compressed to 10–15 minutes. Preventive conversations get cut short. Chronic conditions get managed reactively. And patients feel like a number.

DPC solves this by removing insurance billing from primary care entirely. The practice revenue comes from membership fees, so the physician's incentive is aligned with the patient's health — not with billing volume.

## Do I Still Need Health Insurance with a DPC Membership?

Yes — we recommend it. Your DPC membership covers primary care comprehensively, but it does not cover:
- Emergency room visits
- Hospital stays
- Surgery
- Specialist consultations
- Imaging and advanced diagnostics

Most DPC members pair their membership with a **high-deductible health plan (HDHP)** or a **health-sharing ministry plan** for these situations. Because your primary care is covered by membership, you actually *use* your high deductible less often — meaning lower total healthcare costs overall.

A rough comparison for a healthy adult:

| Option | Monthly Cost (est.) |
|---|---|
| Traditional PPO plan only | $400–$600+/month |
| DPC membership + HDHP | $150–$250/month (combined) |

*Estimates vary widely by age, employer, and plan. Consult a benefits advisor for your specific situation.*

## Is DPC Right for You?

DPC works especially well for:

- **Individuals and families without employer-sponsored insurance** who want excellent primary care at a predictable cost
- **People with chronic conditions** (diabetes, hypertension, thyroid disorders) who need ongoing, attentive management
- **Preventive health-focused patients** who want more time with their doctor to work on root causes
- **Self-employed individuals and small business owners** looking for a cost-effective health benefit
- **Families who want one doctor** who knows every member

DPC may be less ideal if you primarily need specialist-heavy or hospital-based care, or if your employer's insurance plan already fully covers primary care at very low cost.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**What does "direct" mean in Direct Primary Care?**
"Direct" refers to the direct financial relationship between patient and physician — you pay your doctor directly, without an insurance company in the middle. This removes administrative overhead and aligns incentives around your health, not billing volume.

**Is DPC covered by Medicare or Medicaid?**
DPC membership fees are not covered by Medicare or Medicaid. However, Medicare and Medicaid beneficiaries can join a DPC practice as cash-paying members. Specialty and hospital services would still be billed through Medicare or Medicaid as applicable.

**How is DPC regulated?**
DPC is recognized and legal in all 50 states. Many states have passed specific DPC legislation affirming that a DPC membership agreement is not an insurance product and does not require an insurance license to operate.

**Does the monthly fee really cover everything in primary care?**
It covers the vast majority of what most people need from a primary care physician. You only pay extra for labs, medications, and immunizations — and even then, at cost.
