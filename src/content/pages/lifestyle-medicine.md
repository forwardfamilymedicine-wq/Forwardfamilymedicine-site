# Lifestyle Medicine at Forward Family Medicine

## Hero

**Most chronic diseases are preventable — and many are reversible — with the right approach.**

Lifestyle medicine is an evidence-based medical specialty that uses therapeutic changes in nutrition, physical activity, sleep, stress, substance use, and social connection to prevent, treat, and often reverse chronic disease. At Forward Family Medicine, these tools are built into every membership — not offered as an expensive add-on.

---

## What Is Lifestyle Medicine?

Lifestyle medicine is a branch of medicine that recognizes a fundamental truth: the way we live profoundly affects our health. The six pillars of lifestyle medicine — established by the American College of Lifestyle Medicine — are:

1. **Whole-food, plant-predominant nutrition** — eating patterns that reduce inflammation and chronic disease risk
2. **Physical activity** — regular movement as medicine, tailored to your abilities and goals
3. **Restorative sleep** — sufficient, quality sleep as a cornerstone of metabolic and cognitive health
4. **Stress management** — evidence-based techniques to reduce the physiological burden of chronic stress
5. **Avoidance of risky substances** — tobacco cessation, alcohol reduction, and related support
6. **Social connection** — the documented health impact of relationships, community, and belonging

These are not "soft" wellness concepts. They are well-studied clinical interventions. The evidence base for lifestyle medicine spans thousands of peer-reviewed studies — and for many conditions, lifestyle changes outperform medication alone.

## Why Lifestyle Medicine Belongs in Primary Care

Most chronic diseases seen in a family medicine practice — type 2 diabetes, hypertension, obesity, high cholesterol, fatty liver disease, anxiety, insomnia — are strongly driven by lifestyle factors. Yet in traditional primary care, a 10-minute visit rarely allows for a meaningful conversation about nutrition, sleep, or stress.

At Forward Family Medicine, our DPC membership model gives us the time to have those conversations. We treat lifestyle as a clinical tool, not a side note.

> *"We can prescribe the best medications in the world, but if we don't address what's driving the disease, we're always playing catch-up. Lifestyle medicine lets us get ahead of it."* — Dr. Bigley and Dr. Lieberman

## Conditions We Address with Lifestyle Medicine

Lifestyle interventions are most powerful for — but not limited to — the following conditions:

- **Type 2 diabetes and prediabetes** — nutrition and activity interventions can reduce A1C significantly, sometimes eliminating the need for medication
- **Hypertension (high blood pressure)** — dietary changes (DASH/Mediterranean), sodium reduction, and exercise have clinically proven effects
- **Hyperlipidemia (high cholesterol)** — plant-based nutrition patterns and weight management can produce meaningful improvements
- **Obesity and metabolic syndrome** — personalized, sustainable weight management without fad diets
- **Cardiovascular disease prevention** — addressing modifiable risk factors proactively
- **Anxiety and mild-to-moderate depression** — physical activity, sleep optimization, and stress management as evidence-based adjuncts
- **Insomnia** — behavioral sleep medicine (CBT-I principles) before reaching for medication
- **Chronic fatigue and low energy** — often rooted in sleep, nutrition, or stress factors amenable to change
- **Fatty liver disease (NAFLD)** — diet and activity are the primary treatment

## Our Approach to Nutrition

We do not prescribe a single "correct" diet. Instead, we use the best available evidence — which consistently points toward:

- **Plenty of vegetables, fruits, legumes, and whole grains**
- **Adequate lean protein** (plant and/or animal sources depending on your preferences and health goals)
- **Minimizing ultra-processed foods, refined sugar, and excess sodium**
- **Eating patterns that are sustainable for you long-term**

We are familiar with and can help you navigate a range of evidence-informed dietary approaches: Mediterranean, DASH, whole-food plant-based, low-glycemic, time-restricted eating, and others. The best diet is the one you can actually follow.

## Physical Activity as Medicine

Exercise is one of the most powerful interventions available for chronic disease prevention, mood, metabolic health, and longevity. We work with you to build a movement plan that:

- Meets current physical activity guidelines (150+ min/week moderate activity)
- Is appropriate for your current fitness level and any limitations
- Combines aerobic activity with resistance/strength training
- Addresses barriers — because most people know they should exercise more, they just need help making it realistic

We are not personal trainers — we are physicians who understand the clinical evidence and can translate it into practical guidance.

---

## Sleep as a Clinical Priority

Sleep is chronically underemphasized in traditional medicine. At Forward Family Medicine, we treat sleep as a vital sign. Poor sleep is linked to obesity, diabetes, hypertension, immune dysfunction, cognitive decline, and mental health conditions.

We screen for:
- Sleep duration and sleep quality
- Obstructive sleep apnea (and refer for sleep studies when indicated)
- Circadian disruption (shift work, irregular schedules)
- Behavioral factors driving insomnia

We use behavioral approaches (CBT-I principles) as first-line treatment for insomnia before defaulting to sleep medications.

---

## Stress and the Body

Chronic psychological stress has measurable physiological effects: elevated cortisol, immune suppression, worsened glycemic control, increased cardiovascular risk, and more. We take stress seriously as a clinical variable — asking about it directly, validating its impact, and offering concrete strategies including:

- Mindfulness and relaxation techniques
- Cognitive-behavioral approaches
- Exercise and sleep as stress buffers
- Referrals to mental health professionals when appropriate

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Is lifestyle medicine the same as alternative medicine or integrative medicine?**
No. Lifestyle medicine is conventional, evidence-based medicine that uses behavioral and lifestyle interventions — all of which have a strong scientific evidence base. It sits firmly within mainstream medicine and is a recognized specialty of the American Board of Lifestyle Medicine (ABLM).

**Will you just tell me to eat salad and exercise?**
No. We provide individualized, practical, evidence-based guidance tailored to your specific health situation, goals, and life circumstances. One-size-fits-all advice is not part of what we do.

**Can lifestyle changes actually replace medication?**
For some patients with some conditions — particularly prediabetes, mild hypertension, and early metabolic syndrome — lifestyle interventions can achieve results comparable to (or better than) medication, and may allow for medication reduction or elimination. We are honest about when medication is the right tool and when lifestyle is — and often, both work together.

**Is lifestyle medicine covered by my DPC membership?**
Yes — lifestyle medicine counseling and coaching are integrated into your Forward Family Medicine membership at no additional cost. [See full membership details →](/membership)

**Do I have to make dramatic changes overnight?**
Absolutely not. We work incrementally. Small, sustainable changes over time are far more effective — and more durable — than dramatic interventions that don't stick.
