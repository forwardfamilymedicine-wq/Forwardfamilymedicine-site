# What Is a Doctor Meet & Greet?

## Quick Answer

**A doctor Meet & Greet is a short, free, no-obligation appointment — typically 15–20 minutes — where you can meet a physician, see the practice, ask questions, and decide whether it's the right fit before committing to becoming a patient.** It's a standard offering at Direct Primary Care and concierge medicine practices, and it's the recommended first step before joining any membership-based practice.

---

## Why This Matters

Choosing a primary care physician is one of the most important healthcare decisions you can make — but traditionally, patients have had almost no way to evaluate a physician before becoming a patient. You pick a name from an insurance directory, show up for your first sick visit, and hope for the best.

The Meet & Greet changes that. It treats the doctor-patient relationship as what it actually is: a long-term, trust-based partnership that both parties should have a say in forming.

---

## What Happens at a Meet & Greet

A Meet & Greet is a conversation, not a medical appointment. There is no physical exam, no intake paperwork, and no billing. It's typically offered:
- In-person at the practice (preferred, especially if you're switching from another practice)
- By video or phone if you're remote or have a scheduling conflict

**What you can expect:**
- A brief tour of the practice space, if in-person
- An introduction to the physician and any care staff
- A chance to ask any question you have — about the practice model, pricing, services, the physician's approach, or anything specific to your situation
- Honest answers, even if the answer is "this practice might not be the best fit for your needs"

**What you're evaluating:**
- Does this physician seem like someone I can trust over the long term?
- Is this practice genuinely organized to deliver what it promises?
- Does the model (DPC, concierge) fit my health situation and financial picture?
- Do I feel heard in this conversation?

## What to Ask at a Doctor Meet & Greet

Come prepared — this is your interview as much as theirs.

**About the practice model:**
- How many patients are in your panel currently?
- What is the typical wait time for a same-day appointment?
- How do I reach you between visits — phone, text, portal?
- What happens if I need care and you're unavailable?
- How are after-hours urgent questions handled?

**About services and scope:**
- Do you manage [my specific condition]? How?
- Will you prescribe my current medications?
- How do you handle specialist referrals?
- What's your approach to labs — what do they typically cost?

**About the membership:**
- What exactly is included in the monthly fee?
- Are there any services that cost extra?
- What's the enrollment fee and cancellation policy?
- Are there family plans?

**About the physician:**
- What brought you to the DPC model?
- How long have you been practicing?
- What do you enjoy most about primary care?

## Practical Tips

- **Bring a list of your current medications and conditions.** Even in a non-clinical conversation, it helps you get specific answers about whether your situation fits the practice.
- **Ask the questions that matter most to you first.** You may have 20 minutes — know your priorities.
- **Notice the intangibles.** Is the physician making eye contact? Do they seem genuinely interested? Does the office feel organized and calm? These tell you something.
- **Don't feel obligated to decide immediately.** It's appropriate to say "I'd like to think about it" and follow up. A practice worth joining won't pressure you.
- **Compare two or three practices if you're uncertain.** The Meet & Greet makes this easy — it's free and takes 20 minutes.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Do all DPC practices offer free Meet & Greets?**
Most do — it's a standard part of the DPC patient acquisition model. Some concierge practices also offer them. If a practice doesn't offer a free initial consultation of some kind, ask why.

**Is the Meet & Greet only for new DPC patients?**
Typically yes — it's designed for prospective patients who want to learn about the practice before enrolling. If you're already a patient elsewhere and considering switching, this is the right starting point.

**What if I have a complex medical history — is a 20-minute conversation enough?**
It's enough to assess fit. The Meet & Greet is not a comprehensive review of your records — that happens at your first onboarding visit. The purpose is to answer your questions and establish whether this practice can meet your needs. Feel free to summarize the most important aspects of your history so you can get a realistic picture.

**Can I bring my spouse or a family member?**
Absolutely. If you're considering a family plan, it makes sense to bring your partner. Two perspectives make for a better evaluation.
