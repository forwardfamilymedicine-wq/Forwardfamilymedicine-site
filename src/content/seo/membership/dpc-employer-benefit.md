# Direct Primary Care as an Employee Benefit

## Quick Answer

**Small and mid-size businesses can offer Direct Primary Care memberships as a health benefit — giving employees real, accessible primary care while controlling costs better than traditional group health insurance.** DPC employer memberships typically cost $80–$150/month per employee and are often combined with an HDHP, health-sharing plan, or as a standalone benefit for uninsured employees.

---

## Why This Matters

Traditional group health insurance is one of the largest and fastest-growing expense lines for small businesses. Premiums have risen faster than wages for over two decades. Many small employers either can't afford to offer coverage or offer plans with deductibles so high that employees effectively don't use their benefits.

DPC offers a different approach: provide employees with the healthcare they actually use most (primary care) at a transparent, affordable cost — and let a leaner insurance plan cover the rest.

---

## How DPC as an Employer Benefit Works

**Option 1: DPC + HDHP**
The employer covers or subsidizes:
- DPC membership for each enrolled employee
- Contribution toward a high-deductible health insurance plan (for hospital and specialist coverage)

Result: employees have excellent primary care access (DPC) and catastrophic coverage (HDHP), at significantly lower total cost than a traditional PPO.

**Option 2: DPC as a Standalone Benefit**
Some employers offer DPC memberships as a supplemental benefit alongside an existing plan — or as the primary healthcare benefit for seasonal, part-time, or contract workers who wouldn't otherwise qualify for insurance.

**Option 3: DPC + Health Sharing Ministry**
Some employers pair DPC with a health-sharing ministry (not insurance, but a cost-sharing arrangement) instead of HDHP. This is the lowest-cost combination, though it has different coverage characteristics than a regulated insurance plan.

---

## The Employer Value Proposition

### Reduced Sick Days
When employees have a physician they can reach the same day by phone or text, they don't delay care until a small problem becomes a serious one. Faster resolution of acute illness means less time off work.

### Meaningful, Usable Benefit
Many employees on high-deductible plans avoid care because every visit costs real money before the deductible is met. DPC eliminates that barrier for primary care — which is the vast majority of healthcare encounters. Employees actually *use* the benefit.

### Attract and Retain Talent
In competitive hiring markets, offering "DPC membership + HDHP" as a health benefit can differentiate you from employers offering no coverage — at lower cost than a full PPO.

### Predictable, Transparent Costs
Group insurance premiums fluctuate with annual renewals, utilization trends, and carrier decisions. DPC membership fees are fixed, known, and don't change based on how often employees see the doctor.

### Chronic Disease Management Reduces Long-Term Costs
Employees with diabetes, hypertension, or other chronic conditions who receive excellent ongoing primary care are less likely to have expensive acute events, hospitalizations, or disability claims. The ROI on DPC for a workforce with meaningful chronic disease burden can be substantial.

## Practical Considerations for Employers

- **DPC is not a substitute for health insurance.** Employees will still need coverage for hospital, ER, and specialist care. Always pair DPC with a health insurance plan or health-sharing arrangement.
- **Enrollment is voluntary.** Employees choose to participate. Not all employees may find DPC valuable (e.g., those with existing specialist-heavy care relationships).
- **Tax treatment.** Employer-paid DPC membership fees may be treated as taxable compensation to employees under current IRS rules (similar to gym memberships). This is an evolving area — consult your CPA.
- **Panel capacity.** DPC practices have maximum panel sizes. Confirm the practice can accommodate your group before planning.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Can a DPC practice handle a group of 25 employees?**
It depends on the practice's current panel capacity. Forward Family Medicine can discuss group sizes and availability at your initial consultation. We can accommodate groups while maintaining the panel limits that make our model work.

**Do all employees need to enroll, or can we offer it selectively?**
Typically, employers offer DPC as a voluntary benefit that employees can opt into. There's no requirement for universal enrollment.

**What if an employee lives far from the practice?**
DPC practices include telehealth, which handles many primary care needs remotely. For employees who would rarely come in for in-person visits, telehealth makes DPC valuable even from a distance.

**Can DPC be offered through an HRA?**
Some employer HRAs may reimburse DPC membership fees. The IRS rules here are nuanced — work with a benefits attorney or CPA to structure this correctly.
