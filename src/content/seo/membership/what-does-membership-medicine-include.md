# What Does a Membership Medicine Plan Include?

## Quick Answer

**A membership medicine (DPC) plan includes unlimited primary care visits, direct phone/text/message access to your physician, same-day or next-day appointments, annual physicals, preventive screenings, chronic disease management, lifestyle medicine counseling, prescription management, and minor in-office procedures — all for one flat monthly fee.** Labs and medications are typically available at wholesale pricing as an add-on. Specialist care, hospital services, and imaging are not included and require separate coverage.

---

## Why This Matters

The most common question prospective members ask is simply: "What am I actually getting for this monthly fee?" It's a fair question — the DPC model is unfamiliar to many patients, and "unlimited primary care" can sound vague until you see the full list.

The short answer is: everything a primary care physician typically does, with no per-visit charges, plus a level of access and relationship most patients have never experienced.

---

## The Core Inclusions: Visits and Access

### Unlimited Visits — No Per-Visit Fee
Once you're a member, you can see your physician as often as clinically appropriate. Zero additional charge per visit. This is the foundational difference between membership medicine and traditional insurance-based care.

Types of visits included:
- **In-person office visits** — acute illness, chronic care, follow-up, preventive
- **Phone consultations** — for concerns that don't require a physical exam
- **Video visits (telehealth)** — available for appropriate clinical situations
- **Secure message responses** — clinical questions answered via portal or text

### Direct Physician Access
Members contact their physician directly — not a call center, not a nurse-only portal that routes to a callback tomorrow.

- Direct phone number and/or text
- Secure messaging platform (HIPAA-compliant)
- After-hours access for urgent clinical concerns

### Same-Day or Next-Day Appointments
Routine visits are typically available within 24–48 hours. Urgent or acute concerns can usually be seen the same day — or resolved by phone/message without coming in at all.

## Preventive Care

Annual preventive care is fully included in membership:

- **Annual physical exam** — comprehensive history, physical, and lab review
- **Well-woman exams** — Pap smear, breast health, reproductive health discussion
- **Well-child visits** — growth and development, vaccinations, age-appropriate guidance
- **Preventive screenings** — blood pressure, cholesterol, diabetes screening, cancer screenings (per USPSTF guidelines)
- **Immunizations** — available at cost; administration included
- **Health risk assessment** — personalized based on family history and lifestyle factors

---

## Acute (Sick) Care

Acute illness and injury care is included in membership:

- Respiratory infections (colds, flu, bronchitis, sinus, COVID)
- Ear infections, sore throats (including rapid strep testing)
- Urinary tract infections
- Skin conditions (rashes, minor infections, wound care)
- Minor injuries (sprains, strains, lacerations and suturing)
- Gastrointestinal illness
- Minor eye and ear concerns

---

## Chronic Disease Management

Ongoing management of chronic conditions is one of the highest-value inclusions — because it requires *time and continuity* that traditional practices cannot reliably provide.

Included conditions typically managed:
- Type 2 diabetes and prediabetes (A1C monitoring, medication titration, lifestyle counseling)
- Hypertension (medication management, dietary counseling)
- Hypothyroidism and hyperthyroidism
- Hyperlipidemia (cholesterol)
- Asthma and COPD
- Anxiety and depression (screening, medication management)
- Osteoporosis
- Obesity and metabolic syndrome

All follow-up visits, medication adjustments, and lab reviews for these conditions are included in membership at no additional charge.

## Care Coordination

Your membership includes your physician's active coordination of your broader care:

- Specialist referrals with accompanying documentation
- Review of specialist notes and integration into your care plan
- Pre-surgical evaluations and clearances
- Post-hospitalization follow-up care
- Advance care planning

## What Membership Medicine Does NOT Include

To be transparent: a DPC membership covers primary care comprehensively, but not all healthcare.

**Typically charged separately:**
- Laboratory work (priced at wholesale/near-cost — significant savings vs. standard billed rates)
- Prescription medications (wholesale pricing)
- Immunizations (at cost)

**Requires separate insurance coverage:**
- Specialist visits and procedures
- Emergency room care
- Hospital stays and surgery
- Imaging (X-ray, MRI, CT, ultrasound)
- Physical/occupational therapy

Most members pair their DPC membership with a high-deductible health plan (HDHP) to cover these scenarios.

## Practical Tips for Maximizing Your Membership

- **Use telehealth for minor concerns.** A quick message or call often resolves issues faster than coming in — and it's included.
- **Ask about labs before ordering.** DPC lab pricing is dramatically lower than standard billing. It's often worth paying out-of-pocket even if you have insurance.
- **Bring all your health concerns to your annual visit.** There's no cost pressure to leave things out.
- **Contact your doctor proactively.** DPC physicians want to hear from you early — small concerns are easier to address before they become bigger ones.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Is there a visit limit — what does "unlimited" actually mean?**
Unlimited means no cap on the number of visits. Clinically appropriate frequency is determined by your physician based on your needs — you won't be turned away for using the practice "too much."

**Does the membership fee cover family members too?**
Family plans cover a defined group (typically 2 adults + children). Each family member is a full member with all the same inclusions. [See family plan details →](/membership)

**Are specialist visits covered?**
No. Specialist visits are billed through your health insurance. Your DPC physician coordinates specialist care and advocates on your behalf, but the specialist costs are separate.

**What about mental health care?**
Primary care mental health — screening, medication management for depression and anxiety, brief counseling — is included. For therapy and psychiatric care, we provide referrals and coordinate with your mental health providers.
