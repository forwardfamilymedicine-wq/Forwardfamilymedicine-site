**## Quick Answer**
DPC delivers exceptional primary care access, relationship, and cost transparency. Its main limitation is that it doesn't replace insurance — patients still need coverage for specialist and hospital care. For most patients who value accessible, relationship-based primary care, the pros significantly outweigh the cons.

**## The Pros of Direct Primary Care**
_Sections: same-day access, direct physician contact, longer visits, predictable cost, wholesale labs, proactive prevention, unhurried chronic disease management_

**## The Cons of Direct Primary Care**
_Sections: doesn't replace insurance, monthly fee adds to total cost (though often net neutral), panel capacity limits, not ideal for specialist-heavy care needs_

**## Who Benefits Most from DPC**
_Families, chronic disease patients, self-employed, prevention-focused patients_

**## Who Should Think Carefully Before Joining**
